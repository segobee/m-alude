The Beginning of a Long Journey

A casual tea sharing with a friend jolted into gisting about my transition to tech. I always find comfort reminiscing on the back story of my Machine Learning journey.

Beginning of the conversation

Transitioning was cloudy! The bright side of the action I was about to take was kidnapped, or perhaps missing with no existence. The tunnel’s light was severely hazy. I felt so at ease in my own space. However, my long throat and appetite for challenging task and career refined my crude comfort.

My world view about life makes me understand that:

"Peace without challenge will result in chaos of mind, and mind without vision will ply road with so many portholes that will hinder it to get to the destination; though the road to destination is not always smooth."

All these jealously guarded my story. Story aided with enormous questioning. One of such, questioned, how I could make a smooth transition, given that I had previously collaborated closely with those who I would soon be competing with in building with keypads resulting to deep-thinking and solution-driven products. Then comes the unruly prophet of doom and gloom, questioning the number of years of the people in the game. However, my fate in blooming years ahead where I would be part of team(s) that will develop products that would serve as industry standards for solving specific problems and spur industry growth dissuaded that thought.

Mind was made, plans were drawn, and actions were taken. Found myself in the pool of programming and data. The thrilling part is how data solve problems and bring about growth (which is my core strength). The intelligence from data, being the pathfinder for growth, will captain the innovation process to the execution stage garnishing the road to victory, accompany by clear vision.

Into our conversation, now my guy asked:

“How did I make it to this point, learning the guiding principles of Machine Learning?”

To me, answering this question will take me on a journey. I decided to make it succinct by painting an illustration picture. I began the periphery of the story with:

(Note: this is sequel to the cumulative build-up story)

"To every house owner, each one at one point had the picture design of the house they wanted to build in their minds depending on the resources available to them."

This was my case of learning Machine Learning. The picture design of my house was Machine Learning.

"To every high-rise buildings and skyscrapers, foundation is the bedrock. The quality of the soil, the area, structural planning etc, determine how high a building can go."

In my own case, I don’t know how high my building would go (thus, it’s lifelong learning journey). So, I decided to have a rock solid foundation.

Got a land (conceptualized the idea to learn Machine Learning). Proceeded to check the structural planning (formulated my machine learning road map). Did a forensic analysis on the area that host the land which will invariably host the building (mapped out the tools needed to be learned before I can start building Machine Learning solutions). Tested the quality of the soil (deciding the germane tools in building Machine Learning), and some other back-end processes that needed to be followed.

The process began in earnest. The materials for the rock-solid foundation of my building started converging at the site. The likes of:

  • Python: acquired this material through the help of a well-known supplier in the industry — Udemy
  • Numpy: acquired this through the same supplier that supplied the Python material — Udemy
  • Pandas: acquired this through the supplier that previously supplied both Python and Numpy
  • SQL{Postgresql}: Udacity extended a hand of fellowship
  • Advanced Excel: Udemy came to the rescue. Though, the fundamentals of this material had already been secured even before the conceptualization of the building (Machine Learning).
  • Tableau: Udemy came to the rescue again. (The first phase transited to the second phase from here)

Hamoye and the progress

.

.

With the rock-solid foundation secured, I sorted help from a contractor (Hamoye), who at the time was on a mission to build lasting skyscrapers from the ground up (training 21st-century Data Scientists through a rigorous process in solving different industry problems with the inclination for growth).

After Hamoye boarded as the contractor, the building processes began in smooth sailing. They started building from the ground floor leveraging on the already secured materials(Python, SQL, Pandas, Numpy, Tableau, etc.). They designed a four-storey building;

  • Ground floor — Python for Machine Learning
  • First floor — Machine Learning (Regression)
  • Second floor — Machine Learning (Classification)
  • Third floor — Neural Network-Image Recognition & Objection Detection
  • Fourth floor — Practical Time Series Analysis & Forecast

Each floor designed by Hamoye was subsequently evaluated (attached each learning path with a project) together with assessment scores Github Repo. After the evaluation processes, there was team collaboration, testing the designs if they met initial structural planning of the building (Capstone Projects in team collaboration). After the contractual timing of the project elapsed, Hamoye hung their boot after building such a glorious edifice of 4-storey building. My learning with Hamoye concluded here…

I know you would be wondering if that was the end of the building processes. Did I stop my ML learning journey? That’s a question that begs for an answer. But remember, I said earlier that this journey is a life-long journey.

The coming of ALX-T

.

.

One cannot overemphasize the importance of a building of that magnificent to stand the test of time. On this backdrop, it birthed a decision. A decision to comprehensively audit the building constructed by Hamoye (i.e., checking my ML journey through Hamoye and my learning curve if I needed to step back and build on some additional skills that would serve as an armored vehicle paving way for my career progression in Machine Learning).

It will interest you to know that this comprehensive audit led to some hidden discoveries. One of such discovery birthed the need for an interior designer (ALX-T). The mandate of this interior designer is to complement the work that has already been executed by Hamoye and make the building stand strong.

IN MACHINE LEARNING TERM

"The audit opened pool of discoveries. I understood that on rare occasion as ML expert, clean data is not a given. Communication of your findings (insights) through visualization is germane. Then reporting the processes and decisions made while analysing the data is crucial to the problem goal. The discoveries led me to know that some initial acquired skills needed fine-tuning. Data wrangling and visualization seem next shop on my list of ML shopping spree. Who is best suited to undertake this assignment was the question that came to my mind."

"ALX-T lend a hand with their sponsored program through Udacity. This program captured the shortcomings of the previously attained feat. It chronicles into the life processes of Data Wrangling, Visualization, and Reporting. It patches the obvious holes from the earlier program (Hamoye ML). It gives life to data preparation and cleaning where data provided or acquired tend to be dirty. Dutiful bound Model lagged on these intrinsic skills to be able to have predictive power and solve business problem."

Encapsulation of the above skills solidifies the building structure (ML processes). It gives a smooth cruise to level-up on the building

Infrastructural Warehouse

What happens now that all the processes have been completed and the necessary skills have been acquired?

Looking back, we have a standing structure already (acquired skills needed in building business-focused and problem-solving models), what we need more is to elevate the status of the structure.

Let's go back to the factory settings and resume the narration from where we left off.

A high-rise building (four-storey) has been erected, and palliative work has also been done to solidify its base. Building a 21st-century high-rise building is a departure from the old order. Its perspectives and designs are not always conventional, but rather a healthy reworking of the entire process. We’ve reached a point where we need to ensure the building follows through with the modern template. In doing so, we hope to wrap the entire structure in an infrastructure that will ensure its stability in the face of future occurrences and changes.

Google Cloud Platform (GCP) Enters the Journey

.

.

Understanding the earlier position from the business prism was a pointer to adopt additional materials in my quest to gate-keep skills for modern machine-learning workflow. After the areas of focus have been identified, the area to dismantle first surfaces naturally. Over the past years, many cloud computing platforms have come upstream. Judging by recent directions of different businesses after doing their due diligence on the impact of this migration to their businesses and available resources, Azure by Microsoft, AWS by Amazon, and Google Cloud Platform by Google tend to be the first call as the big players in the sector. Different businesses adopt any of those according to the impact on their businesses.

On my part, learning all would be a long stretch. I had to settle for one. Leaning on the back of Google Cloud Platform (GCP) wouldn’t be a bad thing for the start, considering their plethora learning materials. So, I adopted Google cloud.

"It’s very important to know that the world is filled with data, and it comes from all around us. With Machine Learning, meaning can be brought to the data regardless of the source, which will invariably helps in answering questions at scale. Machine Learning application and industries are wide raging. Although, it may not always be apparent, but Machine Learning is everywhere, from image recognition to text and speech system."

The courses and materials provided by Google did tremendous justice to Machine Learning workflow in Google Cloud Platform (GCP). This came on the backdrop of making the Machine Learning tasks better, faster, and easier. Within its infrastructures, there’s presence of Vertex AI Platform, BigQuery ML, TensorFlow, and Keras. Through the well segmented framework of the courses, the following were comprehended:

  • Building and deploying a Machine Learning model using Vertex AI AutoML TensorFlow, and Keras.
  • Understanding the fundamental on when to use AutoML, BigQuery ML, custom training, Vertex AI, and Feature Store.
  • Able to describe Machine Learning products and concept including the Analytics Hub, Dataplex, Data Catalog, predictions and model monitoring, and data quality improvement.
  • Performing exploratory data analysis which translate to building and training ML models leading to it’s implementation via TensorFlow and Keras.
  • Representing and transforming features, understanding the importance of feature engineering, data management, and governance.
  • Using Vizier hyperparameter tuning, monitor ML models, and explain Vertex AI pipelines.

For me, the offshoot of the above courses soft-pedalled the transition of Machine Learning workflow in Google Cloud Platform. Other cloud computing platforms would come after.

Back to the factory setting: all the palliative works have been achieved with a rock-solid foundation.

What’s next? Did my learning continue? Did I continue building on the already established foundation? WAIT FOR IT!
